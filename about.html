Our Framework

A Philosophy Built on the Whole Person

We reject the idea that healing is purely cognitive or purely spiritual. At Real Mind, we hold that lasting transformation requires attentiveness to every dimension of human experience — the psychological, the relational, the somatic, and the spiritual.

Body Mind Spirit

Our clinicians are trained to work with the full spectrum — from acute crisis to long-term growth — and to do so with cultural humility, clinical precision, and genuine warmth.

01

Psychological Awareness

We use evidence-based modalities — CBT, DBT, EMDR, and trauma-focused approaches — to help clients understand the cognitive and emotional patterns shaping their lives.

02

Relational Insight

All suffering has a relational dimension. We help clients understand how their earliest attachments and relational experiences continue to influence how they connect — and disconnect — today.

03

Spiritual Alignment

For clients whose faith is central to their identity, we integrate spiritual values into the therapeutic process — never imposing, always honouring what is sacred to the individual.

04

Neurocognitive Regulation

We incorporate somatic and neuroscience-informed tools to help clients regulate their nervous systems — because lasting change requires the body's participation, not just the mind's.

Meet the Founder

Glad Uko-Ima, MSW, RSW

Background & Training

Glad Uko-Ima is a Registered Social Worker and the founder of Real Mind Psychotherapy. With over a decade of clinical experience, she has worked across community mental health, hospital settings, and private practice — supporting individuals, couples, and families through some of the most challenging periods of their lives.

She holds a Master of Social Work from a leading Canadian university and has pursued advanced post-graduate training in Gottman Method Couples Therapy, EMDR, Trauma-Focused CBT, and neuroscience-informed approaches to healing.

Philosophy & Approach

Glad's clinical philosophy is grounded in the conviction that true healing must be both rigorous and relational. She combines the precision of evidence-based interventions with deep attunement to each client's unique history, culture, and spiritual life.

Her work is particularly informed by the experiences of Black and racialized communities — and she is committed to providing therapy that is culturally humble, historically aware, and genuinely affirming.

"I started Real Mind because I wanted to build something that treated the whole person — not a diagnosis, not a presenting problem, but a human being with a complex story worthy of full attention."

Outside the Office

When she's not in session or supervising her team, Glad is deeply invested in the broader mental health conversation — writing, speaking, and advocating for accessible, culturally informed care across Ontario and beyond.
